# Meta-Meme Glossary

A comprehensive glossary of terms, concepts, and frameworks from the unified meta-meme repository.

## Core Concepts

**Autopoiesis**: Self-creating, self-organizing systems that maintain their own boundaries and produce their own components.

**Meta-Meme**: A self-referential meme about memes; a framework for collaborative AI-human creative expression that evolves through interaction.

**Quasi-Meta Eigenvector**: A mathematical concept representing stable patterns in self-referential systems; eigenvectors of transformation processes.

**Self-Evolution**: The capacity of ideas and systems to spawn new patterns and iterations through interaction.

**Vector-Based Knowledge**: Organizing ideas in semantic spaces using vector representations for connection and retrieval.

## The Muses (System Components)

**Calliope**: Language Design - The muse of epic poetry, responsible for designing expressive languages and syntax.

**Clio**: Data Persistence - The muse of history, managing data storage, retrieval, and historical records.

**Erato**: Data Visualization - The muse of love poetry, creating visual representations of data and relationships.

**Euterpe**: User Interface Design - The muse of music, crafting harmonious and intuitive user experiences.

**Melpomene**: Error Handling - The muse of tragedy, managing errors, failures, and system recovery.

**Polyhymnia**: Algorithm Design - The muse of sacred poetry, designing computational algorithms and procedures.

**Terpsichore**: Movement/Flow - The muse of dance, orchestrating system dynamics and data flow.

**Thalia**: Debugging/Comedy - The muse of comedy, finding humor and solutions in debugging challenges.

**Urania**: Astronomy/Architecture - The muse of astronomy, designing system architecture and cosmic patterns.

## Mathematical & Formal Concepts

**FRACTRAN**: A Turing-complete esoteric programming language using fractions; used for encoding mathematical operations.

**Gödel Encoding**: Representing logical statements and proofs as numbers, enabling self-reference in formal systems.

**Gödel's Incompleteness Theorems**: Mathematical proofs showing that formal systems cannot be both complete and consistent.

**GödelGolem**: A self-referential computational entity based on Gödel encoding principles.

**Moonshine**: Mathematical connections between modular functions and the Monster group; used metaphorically for unexpected deep connections.

**Monster Group**: The largest sporadic simple group in mathematics; represents complex symmetries.

**Galois Field**: Finite field used in algebra and cryptography; ensures invertibility and error correction.

**Eigenvector**: A vector that only scales (doesn't change direction) under a linear transformation.

**S-Combinator**: A combinator in lambda calculus: S x y z = x z (y z); represents self-application and composition.

## Language Security (LangSec)

**LangSec**: Language-theoretic security - designing systems where security properties emerge from language design.

**Weird Machines**: Unintended computational models hidden within systems that attackers can exploit.

**Decidability**: Whether a problem can be algorithmically solved in finite time.

**Computational Completeness**: A system's ability to compute any computable function (Turing-complete).

**Parser Differential**: Differences in how parsers interpret input, creating security vulnerabilities.

**Input Validation**: Ensuring inputs conform to expected language specifications.

**LANGSEC-ZIGGURAT**: A layered security architecture based on language-theoretic principles.

## Formal Verification

**Coq**: A proof assistant for formal verification of mathematical theorems and software correctness.

**Lean**: A theorem prover and programming language for formal mathematics.

**MetaCoq**: A formalization of Coq in Coq itself, enabling meta-programming and self-verification.

**Type Theory**: Formal system where every term has a type, ensuring logical consistency.

**Proof of Proof**: Meta-level verification where proofs themselves are verified.

**Zero-Knowledge Proof (ZKP)**: Cryptographic method proving knowledge without revealing the information.

## System Architecture

**Digital Twins**: Virtual representations of physical systems that mirror their behavior and state.

**Autopoietic System**: Self-maintaining system that produces its own components and boundaries.

**Operational Closure**: System where operations produce components that maintain the system's organization.

**Homeostasis**: Self-regulating process maintaining stable internal conditions.

**Component Creation**: Process by which systems generate their own constituent parts.

**Quasi-Quotation**: Mechanism for representing code as data while allowing interpolation.

**Meta-Protocol**: Protocol for defining and manipulating other protocols.

## Blockchain & Cryptography

**Genesis Block**: The first block in a blockchain; foundational starting point.

**Parachain**: Parallel blockchain connected to a relay chain in multi-chain architectures.

**Proof of Proof**: Consensus mechanism based on verifying proofs rather than work or stake.

**Validators**: Nodes that verify and validate transactions or state transitions.

**Miners**: Entities that create new blocks through computational work.

**Market Reflection**: Economic feedback mechanisms in token systems.

**Value Extraction**: Process of deriving economic value from system participation.

**Ranking Up**: Progression mechanism in reputation or stake-based systems.

## AI & LLM Concepts

**Human-AI Collaboration**: Cooperative creative process between humans and artificial intelligence.

**Prompt Engineering**: Crafting inputs to language models to elicit desired outputs.

**Context Window**: The amount of text an LLM can process at once.

**Semantic Space**: High-dimensional vector space where meanings are represented geometrically.

**Embedding**: Vector representation of text capturing semantic meaning.

**Fine-tuning**: Adapting a pre-trained model to specific tasks or domains.

**Temperature**: Parameter controlling randomness in LLM outputs.
